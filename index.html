About 1M1B

1M1B, 1 Million for 1 Billion, is an open innovation, entrepreneurship and a jobs creation initiative aligned to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) of the United Nations (UN).

VISION

Activating 1 million people for 1 billion people leading to jobs, prosperity and peace. The world is full of opportunities; for some more so than others. We are at a tipping point in global action to level the field for balanced socio-economic progress. While it might sound like a tremendous undertaking, it is indeed possible!

MISSION

Based on the principle "Small is the new big", the people and enterprises that we support address a very specific local issue which potentiates a big social impact in the community. The biggest advantage of 1M1B programs is that it can be customized for people of various cultures and backgrounds, so it remains relevant around the world. 1M1B's goal is to catalyze self-sustaining systems within countries that will continue to foster open innovation, entrepreneurship and job creation for its communities.

Initiatives

Smart Village Gateway

The vision of the Smart Village Gateway is to: Identify critical success elements to develop a Smart Village Evaluate existing technologies being deployed and explore alternative technologies that will lower the operating costs and offer new revenue opportunities for the village Identify the aspects that maybe important to the village communities that are currently not being addressed Evaluate the programs feasibility in the prevalent social, political, and economic dynamic of the villages Identify the weakest links that needs to be addressed to make the program a success

Job Accelerator

The 1M1B Jobs Accelerators focuses on enhancing employment by developing skills and creating innovator’s mindset among local communities. The Jobs Accelerator consists of a 3-5 days ‘Hero’s Mindset’ workshop followed by an Internship & Fellowships program (Changeships) for a selected few on a very specific employment & entrepreneurship opportunity. Example Healthcare Delivery, Business English, Web Designing, and Information Management.

ELEMENTS OF A SMART VILLAGE GATEWAY
TELEMEDICINE CENTER
1M1B Telemedicine centers provides front end and backend infrastructure for medical consultations by certified doctors through an easy to use telemedicine interface facilitated by trained healthcare workers. The center is run by a trained local entrepreneur who anchors the center in their village and catalyzes community mobilization, community engagement and community feedback.
MICRO-ACCELERATOR
The 1M1B Micro-accelerators support small entrepreneurship initiatives that are incubated and accelerated in under-served areas. The enterprises supported in these centers are highly local, and in that sense addresses a very specific challenge but has a big social impact in the community.
WATER AND ENERGY CENTERS
The 1M1B Water and Energy centers include indigenous technologies and frugally developed open innovation solutions from around the world, preferably using an eco-friendly approach. 1M1B implements these solutions in villages and build a use case.
The first Smart Village Gateway is being piloted in the Rudraprayag region, Uttrakhand, India and the East Godavari region, State of Andhra Pradesh, India. For sponsorship opportunities and partnership, write to manav@activate1m1b.org

1M1B JOBS ACCELERATOR
1M1B leverages a unique innovation framework to enhance employability skills like, problem solving, critical thinking, collaboration, team work and effective communication. 1M1B does this by;
1. Hero's Mindset Workshop
• 1M1B leverages a unique innovation framework to enhance employability skills like, problem solving, critical thinking, collaboration, team work and effective communication.
• 1M1B conducts its Hero's Mindset Workshop for diverse audiences around the world. The workshop is typically conducted face to face over a 3-5-day period onsite and helps participants first identify a purpose and then solve problems that are important to their immediate environment and society.

2. Internship and Fellowship for Change (Change-ships)
1M1B's Internships and Fellowships for change (Change-ships) support decisive actions by people for all background and cultures to come together and collaborate to create sustainable prosperity and peace economy. Internships and Fellowships are given to support initiatives for job creation and for mentoring young entrepreneurs in underserved areas.

Our Projects in India are :
1. Tele-Health Centre and Microaccelerator at Mori, Andhra Pradesh
A local school, Dr. Jacques Vigne National School, was selected to host the first 1M1B Microaccelerator with the aim of providing accessible healthcare to the people of the community.
The centre, which started with bi weekly services, soon became a daily feature. A free health check camp was also organized. This included free diagnostic checks and medicine distribution as well.
2. Social Entrepreneurship at Guptkashi, Utttarkhand
3. Entrepreneurship training program, ChangeShips 2016, in partnership with FFE, Bengaluru
1M1B joined hands with Foundation for Excellence (FFE) to provide a platform for young entrepreneurs to develop and showcase their innovative ideas in this year’s ChangeShips program (ChangeShips 2016). It’s goal was to equip the students with the skills of social entrepreneurship over the course of 9 weeks through virtual mentorship.
4. GDA Mentorship Program, in partnership with McKinsey Social Initiative
1M1B Foundation and McKinsey Social Initiative joined hands to execute the ‘GDA Mentorship Program’ in various hospitals in Delhi & NCR over four months as part of McKinsey’s Generations program. What set the project apart is peer mentoring. College students from some of the best colleges in Delhi were trained to be mentors to the new general duty assistants (GDA's) in hospitals across National Capital Region (NCR), India. In spite of the socio-economic divide between the two groups of young individuals, empathy, commitment and sheer grit held the key to the success of this initiative.
5. Workshop on Entrepreneurship for working professionals in partnership with Navbharat Times
6. Women Entrepreneurship Summit 2015
The event, held at YMCA Auditorium in New Delhi on September 12 2015, brought together 200 of the entrepreneurs in-the-making and stalwarts, creating partnership and transforming wannabe entrepreneurs into role models. The Women Entrepreneurship Summit is a platform designed to connect entrepreneurs, innovators and investors and to encourage collaboration and to foster economic opportunity. Both women and youth entrepreneurs had the opportunity to share their stories and inspire upcoming entrepreneurs.
7. Entrepreneurship training program, ChangeShips 2015, in partnership with CDF, Delhi

New York

1 Million 1 Billion executed three programs in the United States of America in 2015-16
1. 1M1B ChangeShips Program 2016
Under the ChangeShips program, students were mentored to generate their business plans and test their ideas in the field. 6 young entrepreneurs attended the virtual weekly sessions over 9 weeks. The program ran simultaneously for students in India and the United States of America. Learning entrepreneurial skills can be life changing for these students who may otherwise not have come across such opportunities. They were mentored and encouraged to launch their ideas as start-ups.

2. Bringing Entrepreneurship to the African-American Community of New York, 2016
1M1B aimed to create more opportunities for the African-American community in New York City who have faced several challenges due to poverty. 15 teams were selected to further undergo a 9 weeks 1M1B accelerator and mentoring program. The Top 3 teams will get the opportunity to showcase their businesses at the 1M1B annual event at the United Nations Headquarters in New York in January 2017.

3. 1M1B ChangeShips Program 2015
Under the ChangeShips 2015 program, students were mentored to generate business plans and tested their ideas in the field over a rigorous 6 months program. The program was simultaneously executed in the United States of America (in New York by Kareem Hertzog, the 1M1B US partner), Caribbean Islands (in partnership with Global Leadership Coalition’s Mary Symmonds), and in India (in partnership with Connecting Dreams Foundation).

1M1B worked with volunteers from Team 4 Tech (a non profit based in Redmond, California) and Orphan Impact, to make children graduating out of orphanages jobs and entrepreneurship ready. Community engagement with the 1M1B Microaccelerator at Guptkashi, Uttarakhand
May 4 2016

The 1M1B team shifted the 1M1B telemedicine center in Guptkashi from its incubation center at JVNS school to the market place on 13th April. New health workers and administrators were trained by the 1M1B fellows to carry out the functioning of the center as well as to build redundancies so that the center can be self-sustainable in the future. The center was shifted to a medical store in the upper market in Guptkashi, functioning as a holistic healthcare unit with consultation and medicine dispensary on the same site. The training was given to 5 female health workers and three male health workers out of which 3 female and one male health worker were employed, and the others were kept as a backup for the future. For promotion of 1M1B telemedicine services in the area, pamphlets were circulated and free consultations were offered on the first day.…
